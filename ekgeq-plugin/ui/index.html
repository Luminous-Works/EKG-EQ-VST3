CUT WIDE, BOOST NARROW
Wide cuts clean up problem areas without removing character. Narrow boosts add presence without harshness.
HIGH-PASS EVERYTHING
Apply a high-pass filter at 80–120Hz on non-bass elements. Removes sub rumble that wastes headroom and muddles the mix.
CUT FIRST, BOOST SECOND
Fix problem frequencies by cutting first. Only boost if the character you want genuinely isn't there. Cutting is more musical.
3kHz IS THE EAR
Human hearing is most sensitive at 2–5kHz. Small boosts here = huge perceived loudness. Cut this range for smooth, distant sounds.
CARDIAC ANALOGUE
Each EKG·EQ band maps to a layer of the cardiac system. Think anatomically — the chest feels sub, the throat shapes mids, the skin is air.
